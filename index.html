The 50/30/20 Rule That Changes Everything

Before you can grow money, you need to understand where it goes. Most teenagers and young adults spend without a plan — and wonder why they're always broke.

The 50/30/20 rule is the simplest way to take control: split your income into needs, wants, and savings every single month.

1
Track every dollar for 30 daysUse your bank app or a free tool like Mint. You can't manage what you don't measure.
2
Set up a "pay yourself first" systemMove 20% to savings the moment money hits your account — before you spend anything.
3
Build a 3-month emergency fund$1,000–$3,000 in a high-yield savings account is your financial seatbelt.
4
Automate, then forget itSet up automatic transfers so saving happens without willpower. Systems beat motivation.
💡 Pro Tips for Teens
Open a high-yield savings account — they pay 10x more interest than regular accounts.
50/30/20 split: 50% needs, 30% wants, 20% saving/investing.
Avoid lifestyle inflation. When your income rises, save the extra first.
Sinking funds = save small amounts monthly for big future expenses.
The latte factor is real. $5/day = $1,825/year — invest that instead.

How $100/Month Becomes $1,000,000

The single most powerful thing a teenager can do: start investing early. Compound interest means your returns earn returns — and time is the key ingredient.

If you invest $100/month starting at age 18, at a 10% annual return (the S&P 500 historical average), you'll have over $1 million by 65.

1
Open a brokerage or Roth IRA accountFidelity, Schwab, or Vanguard. Roth IRA = tax-free growth forever.
2
Start with index funds (S&P 500)VOO or VTI track the top 500 US companies. Own a tiny piece of them all.
3
Set up automatic monthly buysDollar-cost averaging = invest the same amount every month, ignoring market noise.
4
Never panic-sell in a crashDownturns are sales on stocks. Those who held through 2008 and 2020 won big.
📊 Key Investing Terms
Index Fund: A basket of stocks. Low risk, low fees, high returns over time.
ETF: Like an index fund but trades like a stock. VTI, SPY, QQQ are the big ones.
Compound Interest: You earn returns on your returns. Einstein called it the 8th wonder.
Roth IRA: Invest post-tax money. All future growth is 100% tax-free at 60.
Diversification: Don't put all eggs in one basket. Index funds do this automatically.

How the Stock Market Actually Works

A stock is a small ownership stake in a real company. When you buy Apple stock, you literally own a tiny fraction of Apple — its products, profits, and future.

Stock prices move based on supply and demand, driven by company performance, economic news, and market psychology.

1
Understand bull vs bear marketsBull = prices rising. Bear = prices falling 20%+. Both are temporary and normal.
2
Learn to read a live chartOpen the Trading Lab to see real-time charts for Apple, Tesla, NVDA and more.
3
Study real indicatorsSMA, EMA, RSI, MACD — all explained in the Trading Lab with live examples.
4
Practice before real moneyUse the Trading Lab quiz to test your chart reading before you invest a cent.
⚠️ Avoid These Mistakes
FOMO buying: Never buy a stock just because everyone is hyping it on social media.
Day trading without education: 90% of retail day traders lose money. Learn first.
Meme stocks: Most people buy at the top and lose big. Don't be that person.
The winning formula: Low-cost index funds + consistent contributions + long time = wealth.

From Zero to Your First Business

Every billion-dollar company started as someone's side project. The best time to start a business is when you're young — low overhead, high energy, nothing to lose.

1
Find your monetizable skillVideo editing, design, coding, tutoring — if you're good at something, someone will pay.
2
Validate before you buildSell to 3 customers before building your product. Proof of demand = less wasted time.
3
Start lean, then scaleUse free tools: Instagram for marketing, Canva for design, Stripe for payments.
4
Reinvest your first profitsDon't spend early revenue on yourself. Reinvest in marketing, tools, and skills.
🚀 Business Ideas Under $100
Freelance service: Design, video editing, web dev on Fiverr or Upwork.
Content creation: YouTube, TikTok — build an audience and monetize.
Reselling: Flip thrift store finds on eBay, StockX, Depop.
Print-on-demand: Design t-shirts on Printify — no inventory needed.
Tutoring/coaching: Get paid to teach what you already know.
